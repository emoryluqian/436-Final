Motivation & Background

In modern day, there continue to be great strides taken towards accommodating those with disabilities: ranging from the requirement of wheelchair accessible entries in public buildings, to webpage support for screen readers continuing to grow. However, one area that has failed to make a widespread push towards accessibility is videogames, particularly in the physical sense.

In 2017, Laurie Malong posted a scholarly article “Assessment of Active Video Gaming Using Adapted Controllers by Individuals With Physical Disabilities: A Protocol” stated that due to this inaccessibility, many disabled individuals live more sedentary lives than is healthy, but it can be fixed if they gain access to movement based games through accommodations. This study was based around allowing kids access to Active Video Games that they would not originally be able to play due to their physical disabilities. Ultimately the study proved that accessibility proved for great physical health increase for those who initially could not play, and extrapolating from that normal people who lack the physical space for these things could also benefit from it.

With this information in mind, we were motivated to create a desk-used game board made up of large buttons emulating the arrow keys on a keyboard in order to encourage people with certain foot-related disabilities to experience games such as Dance Dance Revolution which requires foot movement to navigate and play.
