Motivation & Background

In modern day, there continue to be great strides taken towards accommodating those with disabilities: ranging from the requirement of wheelchair accessible entries in public buildings, to webpage support for screen readers continuing to grow. However, one area that has failed to make a widespread push towards accessibility is videogames, particularly in the physical sense.

In 2017, Laurie Malong posted a scholarly article “Assessment of Active Video Gaming Using Adapted Controllers by Individuals With Physical Disabilities: A Protocol” stated that due to this inaccessibility, many disabled individuals live more sedentary lives than is healthy, but it can be fixed if they gain access to movement based games through accommodations. This study was based around allowing kids access to Active Video Games that they would not originally be able to play due to their physical disabilities. Ultimately the study proved that accessibility proved for great physical health increase for those who initially could not play, and extrapolating from that normal people who lack the physical space for these things could also benefit from it.

With this information in mind, we were motivated to create a desk-used game board made up of large buttons emulating the arrow keys on a keyboard in order to encourage people with certain foot-related disabilities to experience games such as Dance Dance Revolution which requires foot movement to navigate and play.

Product Materials

Foam board, adhesive paper, push buttons, jumper wires, Rasberry pi board

Product Introduction

The TENET Board is a desk-used game board inspired by Dance Dance Revolution pad and made up of large buttons emulating the arrow keys on a keyboard in order to encourage people with certain foot-related disabilities to experience games such as Dance Dance Revolution which requires foot movement to navigate and play.

The case is made of foam board and covered by adhesive paper. Five push buttons go through the case and connected to Rasberry pi board by jumper wires. When generating codes during building phase, we connected the Rasberry pi board to a computer interface and modified codes. However, when use it regularly, no computer interface is needed. All it needs just a monitor, that's why when designed future work, we added a projector to this product.

Product Future Work

For future work, we would like to add LED lighting to each button with different colors; this way, people can use it in the dark without having any trouble. We would also like to add a bluetooth featuer and two arm sensors to read heart rate, and the Rasberry pi can gether information to generate a health report and send to connected phone automatically via bluetooth, so users can keep track of their health situation. The last thing we would like to add is a projector. Ideally, a holographic laser projector. In this way, a monitor is not necessary anymore, or the projector can be used as a larger monitor. Taping the arm sensor (either one) twice quickly can turn off the projector, repeat to turn it on.
